On June 10, 2021, shortly after midnight, Central District officers responded to the 300 block of Kennedy Street for reports that someone had been shot.

Upon arrival, officers located a male lying outside who had been shot and was seriously injured. They provided emergency first-aid to this male; until he was transported by ambulance to hospital in critical condition, where he later succumbed to his injuries.

The deceased victim has been identified as Marlon Jose CHAMORRO-GONZALES, 48, of Winnipeg.

This investigation is continuing by members of the Homicide Unit. Anyone with information that may assist investigators is asked to call the Homicide Unit 204-986-6508 or Crime Stoppers at 204-786-TIPS (8477).

Stabbing â Investigation: C21-132964

On June 10, 2021, at approximately 1:00 a.m., Central Patrol officers were guarding a scene in the area of Ellice Avenue and Kennedy Street for what was later determined to be the homicide mentioned in the above release.

An adult male not related to the scene who had been stabbed approached the officers and requested their assistance. The officers provided emergency first-aid to the 47-year-old victim, who was subsequently transported to hospital by ambulance in unstable condition. He was later upgraded to stable condition; however, he could not provide any suspect information or details of the assault at this time.

This investigation is ongoing; anyone with information is asked to contact the Major Crimes Unit at 204-986-6219. Additional information can be relayed anonymously to Crime Stoppers at 204-786-TIPS (8477).

Assault with a Firearm â Investigation: C21-132989

On June 10, 2021, at approximately 2:30 a.m., East District officers responded to the 100 block of Henry Avenue, for the report of a male who had been shot.

The 52-year-old victim had been along the riverbank near the Disraeli Bridge when he heard shots and realized that he had been struck. He could not provide any suspect information. Officers provided first-aid, and he was transported by ambulance to hospital in stable condition.

This investigation is ongoing; anyone with information is asked to contact the Major Crimes Unit at 204-986-6219. Additional information can be relayed anonymously to Crime Stoppers at 204-786-TIPS (8477).

Theft of Emergency Vehicle â Arrest: C21-132472

On June 9, 2021, at approximately 2:00 p.m., the Winnipeg Police Service responded to the report of an ambulance that had been stolen from the Health Sciences Centre Winnipeg parking area assigned to Emergency vehicles.

A male approached an ambulance from the Brandon region, which was parked in the loading area. He requested assistance from the paramedics who were inside the ambulance at the time. He immediately climbed in through the back doors and made his way to the driver's seat. The paramedics exited the ambulance for their safety, and the suspect drove away, stealing the ambulance.

He drove to a parking lot in the 1500 block of Wall Street and crashed into a parked vehicle, where he abandoned the ambulance and fled on foot and ran to the adjacent 1300 block of Downing Street. While in this neighbourhood, he continued his erratic behaviour by breaking some windows of residences and stole an axe from one of the yards. One of the homeowners confronted the suspect, who then attempted to assault him by swinging the axe, missed and then fled again on foot.

Officers searching the area apprehended him while still armed with the axe in a residential backyard, where he was safely taken into custody. Nobody was physically injured.

Jordan Nolan MCDOUGALL, 34, of Winnipeg, was charged with:

- Theft of Motor Vehicle
- Theft Under $5,000
- Mischief Under $5,000 x 2
- Identity Fraud
- Fail to Comply with Condition of Release Order
- Possession of a Weapon
- Assault with a Weapon
- HTA driving offences x 2

He was detained in custody.
