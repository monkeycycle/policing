Winnipeg Police Service

January 14, 2019

Winnipeg Police Service Media Release
For Immediate Release

Animal Cruelty – Arrest: C19-2210

On January 4, 2019, at approximately 5:05 a.m., two East District General Patrol officers attended to the 700 block of Home Street while following up on an unrelated matter. After exiting their cruiser car, the two officers noticed a strong smell coming from a residence.

The officers, deciding to investigate, approached the house and observed a number of dogs barking and clawing at the window. As they got closer, 15 dogs were observed inside the residence. The dogs appeared to be both malnourished and in distress.

Emergency assistance was provided by the City of Winnipeg’s Animal Services Agency. Inside the house, deplorable conditions were found including no food or water and a substantial build-up of urine and feces. It appeared that the dogs had been abandoned and had begun to consume parts of couches, beds and walls.

Animal Services Officers, with the assistance of General Patrol police officers, seized the 15 dogs and transported them to a City of Winnipeg facility where they received medical care. The dogs continue to receive treatment at this time and will be placed up for adoption at a later date.

Central District General Patrol officers continued with the investigation and on January 12, 2019, a female was placed under arrest at a residence in the Richmond West area.

Crystal Marie Molloy, a 35-year-old female of Winnipeg, has been charged with:

- Inflict upon an animal acute suffering, serious injury or harm, or extreme anxiety or distress that significantly impairs its health or well being (Animal Care Act)
- Fail to ensure adequate source of food and water for an animal (Animal Care Act)
- Confine an animal to an enc losure or area with unsanitary conditions, so as to significantly impair the animal's health or well-being (Animal Care Act)
- Causing Unnecessary Suffering to an Animal (Criminal Code)
- Abandon or Neglect Animal (Criminal Code)

It is important the residents provide proper food, water and shelter for their pets. The legal limit for dogs is 4 in Winnipeg.

Don’t let yourself get overwhelmed and spay/neuter your pets. If you need help, reach out to friends, family, animal shelters, rescues and or social services. For more information on accessing social services, please visit: http://mb.211.ca/

Suspected animal cruelty or neglect should be reported to the Provincial Animal Care Line at 204-945-8000.

More information on reporting suspected animal cruelty or neglect can be found here:
https://www.gov.mb.ca/agriculture/animals/animal-welfare/animal-care-and-handling.html

Weapons — Arrests: C19-8927

On January 13, 2019, at approximately 2:30 a.m., Winnipeg Police received a report of shots being fired from a taxi in the area of King Street and Flora Avenue. The driver of the taxi indicated that the suspects were passengers and recently dropped off in the area.

North District general patrol, K-9, and Tactical Support Team officers attended and located several people in a residence; they were taken into custody. A search of a second residence in the area was conducted and a loaded sawed-off 12 gauge shotgun was seized.

Officers learned the taxi was transporting three people when the front passenger produced a firearm and began to wave it around; at times pointing it at the driver. The driver continued to drive and in the area of King Street and Selkirk Avenue, the front passenger leaned out the window and fired one shot. Shortly after, all passengers were dropped off at their requested destination at which time the passenger fired a shot through the front windshield before exiting the taxi.

The taxi driver did not require medical attention.

Dorian Tyrell Packo, a 19-year-old male of Winnipeg has been charged with:

- Possess Firearm, Restricted/Prohibited Weapon, or Ammunition Contrary to Prohibition Order (x4)
-Possess Firearm Knowing its Possession is Unauthorized (x2)
-Careless Use of Firearm, Weapon, Prohibited Device or Ammunition
-Pointing a Firearm
-Possess Weapon (x2)
-Carry Concealed Weapon, Prohibited Device or Ammunition (x2)
-Unauthorized Possession of a Firearm in Motor Vehicle
-Possession of a Loaded Prohibited or Restricted Firearm (x2)
-Discharge a Restricted Firearm or a Prohibited Firearm with Intent
-Unauthorized Possession of a Firearm

Rudy Carpentier, a 24-year-old male of Winnipeg has been charged with:

- Possess Firearm, Restricted/Prohibited Weapon, or Ammunition Contrary to Prohibition Order
- Possess Firearm Knowing its Possession is Unauthorized
- Possess Weapon
- Possession of a Loaded Prohibited or Restricted Firearm
-Careless Use of Firearm, Weapon, Prohibited Device or Ammunition
-Unauthorized Possession of a Firearm

Both males have been detained at the Winnipeg Remand Centre.

An 24-year-old male and a 19-year-old female were also arrested for unrelated outstanding warrants.

Aggravated Assault — Arrest: C18-223856 & C19-7704

On October 10, 2018, at approximately 2:55 a.m., Winnipeg Police responded to a report of an injured male in the 200 block of Selkirk Avenue.  A 56-year-old male was located with significant upper body injuries and transported to hospital.

Members of the Major Crimes Unit investigated and identified a suspect. It was learned that the suspect was at the victim's residence when he struck the victim several times with a hammer. As a result, the victim has suffered life-altering injuries.

The suspect could not be located and a warrant was issued for his arrest.

On January 11, 2019, North District general patrol officers located the suspect in the area of Magnus Avenue and Aikins Street after he stole two bottles of soda for a nearby convenience store. He was taken into custody.

Justin Emile Pelletier, a 22-year-old male of Winnipeg, has been charged with:

- Aggravated Assault
- Theft Under
- Fail to Comply with Probation Order

He has been detained at the Winnipeg Remand Centre.

Theft/Assault Police Officer – Arrests: C19-9286

On January 13, 2019, at approximately 5:35 p.m., East District General Patrol members, along with the K9 Unit, responded to the report of a theft at a Liquor Mart in the 900 block of Henderson Highway.

A number of youth suspects had entered the business and proceeded to knock bottles of liquor onto the floor, causing them to break. They then began to steal various bottles of liquor and fled a short time later. Approximately $740 in liquor was stolen.

A first male suspect was located in the area of Brazier Street and Hazel Dell Avenue by General Patrol members. The male refused to comply with police demands and after a brief struggle, was taken into custody. A quantity of stolen liquor was recovered. He later threatened to kill two officers and continued to be violent.

A second male suspect was located in the area of Henderson Highway and Helmsdale Avenue after a foot pursuit by General Patrol members. During the foot pursuit, the male threw a bottle of liquor at an officer, narrowly missing him. The male was taken into custody and a quantity of stolen liquor was recovered.

A third suspect was located hiding in a yard in the 200 block of Helmsdale Avenue after a lengthy track by K9 Unit members.

A 14-year-old male of Winnipeg has been charged with:

- Mischief Under $5,000/Obstruct in Enjoyment
- Assault
- Uttering Threats - Cause Death or Bodily Harm (x2)
- Possession of Property Obtained by Crime Under $5000
- Theft Under $5000
- Fail to Comply with Sentence

He was also processed on an outstanding arrest warrant for:

- Fail to Comply with Sentence (x2)

He was detained in custody.

A 16-year-old of Winnipeg has been charged with:

- Assault a Peace Officer with a Weapon
- Mischief Under $5,000/Obstruct in Enjoyment
- Fail to Comply Condition Undertaking by Judge/Justice
- Theft Under $5000

He was detained in custody.

A 16-year-old male of Winnipeg has been charged with:

- Theft Under $5000

He was released on a Promise to Appear.
