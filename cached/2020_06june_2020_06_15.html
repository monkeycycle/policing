June 15, 2020 Media Release

Stabbings

On June 12, 2020, at 3:35 p.m., members of the Winnipeg Police Service responded to a residential building in the 300 block of Hargrave Street for a report of a stabbing (incident C20-135306).

Upon arrival, officers located an injured 60-year-old male. He was transported to hospital in stable condition. It is believed that the incident occurred when a male sought out retaliation for a broken window and came upon an unsuspecting group of people. He subsequently threatened the group and stabbed one individual.

Nicholas Leal Matos, a 19-year-old male, has been charged with:

- Assault with a Weapon (x2)
- Uttering Threats - Cause Death or Bodily Harm (x4)
- Mischief Under $5,000/Obstruct in Enjoyment

On June 13, 2020, at 12:24 a.m., members of the Winnipeg Police Service responded to a residence in the Richmond West neighbourhood for a report of a stabbing (incident C20-135737).

Upon arrival, officers located a severely injured 17-year-old male. He was transported to hospital in unstable condition. It is believed the incident occurred after the 17-year-old attempted to rob a 14-year-old male acquaintance, who retaliated with a knife and stabbed him several times.

As previously released:

On May 30, 2020, at 11:30 a.m., Winnipeg Police responded to a report of a male who had been assaulted in the 500 block of Selkirk Avenue. Responding officers located a 15-year-old male on the sidewalk suffering from serious head trauma. He was conveyed to hospital in critical condition where he remains with what are expected to be life-altering injuries.

Investigators believe the attack was unprovoked and that one male suspect is responsible.

Update:

Members of the Major Crimes Unit continued with the investigation. It is believed that the suspect and victim were previously unknown to each other and had been waiting at a restaurant when they became engaged in a conversation. The suspect then showed the victim a firearm which he had concealed in his backpack. The victim received his food and began to walk away, at which time the suspect struck him with a hammer causing catastrophic injuries. Investigators have not identified a motive.

On June 14, 2020, at approximately 1:04 p.m., the suspect was placed under arrest in the area of Austin Street North and Pritchard Avenue. He was found to be in possession of a knife and hatchet.

Jerry Robert Kipling, a 30-year-old male of Winnipeg, has been charged with:

On June 12, 2020, at 11:48 p.m., General Patrol members, along with AIR1 and the K9 Unit, responded to a residence in the first 100 block of Celina Cove for a report of a break-in.

AIR1 subsequently located three suspects as they ran through yards and attempted to hide. They were taken into custody soon after.
