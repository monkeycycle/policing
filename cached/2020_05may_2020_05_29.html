Enforcement of Reduced Speed School Zones

On Monday, March 23, 2020, due to the suspension of schools and closure of playgrounds, the Winnipeg Police Service advised that reduced speed school zones, those that reduce to 30 km/h from Sept-June, 0700-1730hrs, would not be enforced.

Effective Monday, June 1, 2020, school operations will resume in a limited capacity and playgrounds are open. As a result, the Winnipeg Police Service, along with the Photo Enforcement Unit, will resume enforcement of the Reduced Speed School Zones.

There will be an increase in children in the area as they attend at unpredictable times based on appointments in the school, and with improved weather, they will also be more active in the playgrounds.

Investigators believe that the homicide was premeditated and gang-related. The victim had been socializing in the area when he was shot as he returned to the building he lived in. It was also determined the building had been shot at three days earlier as well.

On May 17, 2020, Winnipeg Police responded to a report of a Sexual Assault that had occurred earlier that day in Kildonan Park. 

The victim, a woman in her thirties, was in the park with her child when a male sat down beside her and struck up a conversation. While speaking to the woman, he groped her. The victim left immediately with her child. She was not physically injured. 

An investigation by North District General Patrol officers identified a suspect. On May 28, officers arrested a 34-year-old male who lived near the park. A charge of Sexual Assault is pending.
