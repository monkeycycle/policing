Winnipeg Police Service Media Release
For Immediate Release

MISSING PERSON – Cynthia FORSTER – C19-30140 - Located

The Winnipeg Police Service is requesting the public’s assistance in locating a missing 18-year-old female, Cynthia FORSTER. 

FORSTER was last seen in the North End area of Winnipeg on the evening of February 10th, 2019 at 6:30 pm.

FORSTER is described as Indigenous in appearance, 5’8” in height, 180lbs, long brown hair with red. No clothing description.

The Winnipeg Police Service is concerned about FORSTER'S well-being and is asking anyone with any information regarding her whereabouts to contact the Winnipeg Police Service Missing Persons Unit at 204-986-6250.

Kick Off for the Foodfare — WPS Half Marathon, 2-Person Relay & 5K

WPS participants looking to top $2 million life-saving cancer research

Justice Minister Cliff Cullen, Mayor Brian Bowman, and Superintendent Scot Halley join forces today urging runners and walkers from all levels to come together and make this year's 15th Anniversary Foodfare — Winnipeg Police Service Half Marathon, 2-Person Relay and 5K Run the biggest and best yet.

This year's race is set to break the $2 million fundraising mark; the message from all leaders was simple: by working together, life can be BIGGER than cancer.

Registration for the 15th-anniversary race kicks off today at Université de Saint-Boniface where Dr. Ron Geyer will explain how his team is on the verge of something ground-breaking in the fight against glioblastoma, one of the toughest and deadliest forms of brain cancer.  This same cancer that took the lives of Tragically Hip frontman Gord Downie and long-time WPS runner, Joanne Schiewe, just a few years ago.

“It is the most common form of brain cancer and it’s got a dismal 5-year cure rate of less than 10% and so we know the urgency of making progress,’’ Dr. Geyer said. “It’s because of organizations like the Canadian Cancer Society and events like the WPS Half Marathon that my team has been able to focus our efforts in the lab. Dr. Geyer`s team is one of several cancer research projects funded in part by the WPS Half Marathon.

In its first 10 years, the half marathon raised its first $1 million in support of research and programs that support Manitoba cancer patients. Four years ago, the race organizers focused fundraising efforts on one of the toughest forms of cancer – brain cancer. Every year about 3,000 Canadians are diagnosed with brain cancer and 2,400 people in the country die from it. It is the most common form of cancer death among young children.

A partnership between the Canadian Cancer Society and Brain Canada, a public-private partnership established by the Government of Canada, means that a pledge made to a WPS Half Marathon runner would be matched dollar-for-dollar by Brain Canada. 

Four years later, the half marathon is just $163,000 away from raising it's second million dollars.  

“What a legacy of giving by the Manitoba running community,” said Dan Holinda, Prairie Regional Executive Director of the Canadian Cancer Society. “On behalf of the 30,000 Manitoba families living with cancer right now, I want to thank the Winnipeg Police Service, Race Director Nick Paulet and his amazing Cops for Cancer Race Committee for their unwavering support of cancer patients and the Canadian Cancer Society.

The race will again start and finish inside Assiniboine Park, near the baseball diamonds. There will be starts for the half marathon and 2-person relay and a separate start for the 5K. The event is open to runners and walkers of all skill levels. The course will remain open for 4 hours to allow runners, wheelchair athletes and walkers to participate.

Participants can register online at WPShalfmarathon, in person at any Running Room location, or at the Canadian Cancer Society office at 193 Sherbrook Street. Regular registration is available until March 11th. Free registration is available for those raising funds to fight brain cancer.

More information about the cancer society is available at cancer.ca.

Armed and Barricaded – Public Mischief Charges: C19-27292

On February 7, 2019, at approximately 6:00 a.m., Winnipeg Police responded to a report of a male with a firearm in the 300 block of Blake St. The caller advised that a male had banged on her apartment door while armed with a firearm. She advised that this male asked her to conceal the firearm, and a quantity of drugs within her suite.

The female caller provided responding officers with detailed descriptions of the alleged assailant, the rifle, and her interaction with the male. This female was adamant that the male was still with the neighbouring suite.

As a result of the extremely serious risk posed within this multi-family residential unit significant WPS resources were deployed, some of which included;

- 5 Tactical Support teams
- 1 K9 unit
- 6 General Patrol units
- 1 Tactical Emergency Medical Services team (WFPS)
- WPS robot
- WPS Incident Supervisor
- Dedicated WPS emergency dispatcher
- Various support personnel from the Province of Manitoba

After approximately 4 ½ hours police were able to confirm the suspect was not present, and no firearm was located. A review of security video footage confirmed there was no armed male, and in fact, no person whatsoever had come to the caller's door. As a result it was determined that the incident was intentionally fabricated.

On February 13, 2019, police arrested the 18-year-old female caller and charged her with Public Mischief (CCC 140(1)). She was released on an Appearance Notice.

The Winnipeg Police Service reminds the public of the serious consequences of making a false report that causes police to enter into an investigation based on a false or misleading statement.
