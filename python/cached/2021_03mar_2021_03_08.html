As previously released:

On March 4, 2021, at 5:59 p.m., members of the Winnipeg Police Service responded to Main Street and Alfred Avenue for a report that possible human remains had been found in the area.

Upon arrival, officers confirmed the remains were that of a deceased individual. The deceased’s age and identity are unknown at this time, and a post-mortem examination has been scheduled.

This investigation is continuing by members of the Homicide Unit.

Update:

The deceased victim has been identified as Jana Noella Williams, a 28-year-old female of Winnipeg.

Members of the Homicide Unit are asking the public for their help with establishing Jana’s whereabouts and activities over the past two weeks. They are also hoping to speak with anyone that she had recently spent time with.

We are providing both images in an attempt to help with the investigation. Anyone who recognizes Jana and has information regarding her recent whereabouts is asked to contact police.

On March 6, 2021, at 6:22 a.m., members of the Winnipeg Police Service began responding to the area of Main Street and Burrows Avenue after several callers to 911 reported a female walking in traffic with a large knife. The female was acting erratically and threatening bystanders with the weapon.

On March 6, 2021, at 5:25 p.m., members of the Winnipeg Police Service began responding to the 1000 block of Main Street after 911 callers reported a male pointing a firearm at people and buildings in the area. The male had also been in dispute with a group of people earlier.

A Tactical Support Team unit arrived in the area and located the male at a nearby bus shelter with several other individuals. Officers engaged the male and were able to deescalate the matter, at which time the male discarded the weapon and was safely taken into custody.

The weapon was determined to be a .177 calibre CO2 pistol designed to look like a Makarov semi-automatic handgun replica.

Note: In the attached pictures, the grip has been moved to the side, and the CO2 cartridge has been removed. A surveillance image showing the incident has also been provided.

CLARKE is expected to reside in Winnipeg, Manitoba.
