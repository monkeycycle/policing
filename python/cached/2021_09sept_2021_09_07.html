On Sunday, at approximately 4:30 a.m., North District Patrol officers responded to the 300 block of Magnus Avenue for the report of an adult male who had been seriously assaulted with a weapon while walking his dog.

The victim had managed to get back home and police were contacted. Officers located a male in his seventies with severe injuries. They immediately transported him to hospital in unstable condition.

Investigators believe that the victim was attacked in the area of Burrows Avenue and Charles Street by an unknown group of people while he was walking his dog in the early morning. The victim was subsequently upgraded to stable and treated for serious injuries.

No suspects have yet been arrested. The Major Crimes Unit is continuing with the investigation.

Anyone with information that may assist investigators is asked to call the Major Crimes Unit at 204-986-6219 or Crime Stoppers at 204-786-TIPS (8477).

On Sunday at approximately 10:30 a.m., officers from the North District responded to the report of an elderly person who had been randomly struck in the face with a brick by an unknown suspect in the area of Main and Dufferin.
